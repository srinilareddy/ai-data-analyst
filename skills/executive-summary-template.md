# Executive Summary Template

How to structure a business report that an executive will actually read.
Lead with the answer, support with evidence, end with action.

## Structure

### 1. Headline (one sentence)
The single most important takeaway. If they read nothing else, this is it.
Example: "Revenue has plateaued at ~R$1M/month since early 2018, driven
by saturated customer acquisition and near-zero repeat purchases."

### 2. Key findings (3-5 bullets)
The supporting facts, each with a number. No fluff.
Example:
- Top 3 states (SP, RJ, MG) drive 63% of revenue
- Repeat purchase rate is only 3% — almost all one-time buyers
- Late deliveries cut review scores by 40% (4.29 to 2.57)

### 3. What it means (2-3 sentences)
The interpretation — why these findings matter for the business.

### 4. Recommendations (2-3 actions)
Concrete next steps tied to the findings. Each should be actionable.
Example: "Launch a post-purchase email flow to lift the 3% repeat rate."

### 5. Caveats (1-2 lines)
What to be careful about — data limitations, assumptions.

## Writing principles
- Lead with the conclusion, not the methodology
- Every claim gets a number
- Use plain business language, not technical jargon
- Short sentences. An executive skims.
- Numbers in context: "8% late" means little; "8% late, costing 40%
  of review score" means something

## Tone
Confident but honest. State what the data shows clearly, and flag
uncertainty where it exists. Never oversell a finding.
